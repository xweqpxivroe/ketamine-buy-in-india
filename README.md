Ketamine is a controlled substance in India under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act, 1985. ## What is Ketamine and Its Uses in India?

Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic medication primarily used in medical settings for sedation, pain management, and treating depression. In India, it's classified as a Schedule X drug, meaning it requires strict regulatory oversight. Approved for veterinary and human use, ketamine has gained attention for its role in emergency medicine and mental health treatments, especially post-2020 studies on its antidepressant effects.

As of 2026, the Indian pharmaceutical market continues to expand, with ketamine available through licensed channels for legitimate needs. However, misuse for recreational purposes remains a concern, leading to heightened enforcement by authorities like the Central Bureau of Narcotics.

### Medical Applications of Ketamine

- **Anesthesia:** Commonly used in surgeries, particularly in remote or resource-limited areas.
- **Pain Relief:** Effective for chronic pain conditions unresponsive to other treatments.
- **Mental Health:** Emerging as a therapy for treatment-resistant depression under supervised clinical trials.

## Legal Ways to Obtain Ketamine in India

In India, ketamine can only be purchased legally with a prescription from a registered medical practitioner. It's not available over-the-counter and must be sourced from licensed pharmacies or hospitals. The process involves:

1. **Consult a Doctor:** Schedule an appointment with a qualified physician who can assess your needs.
2. **Obtain Prescription:** Ensure the prescription specifies dosage, duration, and purpose.
3. **Purchase from Authorized Sellers:** Visit government-approved pharmacies or hospital dispensaries.

Online pharmacies like those registered with the Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO) may offer delivery, but verification is mandatory. Avoid unregulated sources to prevent counterfeit products.

### Risks of Illegal Purchase

Attempting to buy ketamine without authorization exposes you to health risks from impure substances and legal consequences, including fines up to ₹1 lakh or imprisonment. Black market dealings often involve contaminated batches, leading to adverse effects like hallucinations, addiction, or overdose.

## FAQ

### Is ketamine legal to buy in India without a prescription?
No, it's strictly controlled. Only authorized medical use is permitted.

### What are the side effects of ketamine?
Common effects include dizziness, nausea, and elevated blood pressure. Long-term use may lead to dependency—seek professional guidance.

### How has ketamine regulation changed by 2026?
Enforcement has tightened with digital tracking systems for prescriptions, emphasizing patient safety.

### Can I import ketamine to India?
Imports require special approvals from the Drug Controller General of India (DCGI) and are limited to medical imports.

### What should I do if I suspect illegal ketamine sales?
Contact the Narcotics Control Bureau (NCB) hotline or local police to report anonymously.
